Working with functions

Using the various functions covered, complete the following tasks:

Work with this spreadsheet

In trump_clinton_join:

  • join trump and clinton data into one spreadsheet
  • join the latitude and longitude columns into one lat_long column (like this: 43.649000,-79.378110)

In nhl_player_salaries:

  • find the total salary for the 2018-2019 NHL season
  • who was paid the most? who was paid the least?
  • what is the most common salary, what is the average salary
  • insert a new column; populate it with the total salary each player will earn from 2018-2023
  • what is the average total salary?

In weather:

  • use past transpose to return table data where the days of the week are in a column
  • add column headers
  • use function that returns 1 if the forecasted temperature is above 0, and returns 0 for weather below freezing
  • get a total of the days above freezing
  • in a new column, return 1 if the description includes the word ‘sunny’, and 0 if it does not (you will need multiple functions)
  • get a total of the ‘sunny’ days

In best_selling_books:

  • how many of the best selling books were published in English?
  • get the total Approximate sales
  • how many Harry Potter books made this list?
  • count how many books sold more than 75 million copies
  • what is the most common genre
  • in which decade were the most titles published?
  • who is the most popular author, by the number of books sold (how many books did they sell)

BONUS in math from the previous exercise

  • write a function, or series of functions, that checks if a movies is a sequel
  • what percent of the highest grossing movies are sequels (extra bonus: what is the percent change from 2017?)
  • hint: movies that are sequels includes either, a number (2), or a colon (:) and the subtitle
